Movies and shows are rated fairly

  • CARA stands for Classification & Ratings Administration
  • According to a survey taken July of 2016:
  1. 75% find the ratings useful
  2. 85% find th rating descriptors useful
  • MPAA stands for Motion Picture Association of America
  • The rating board considers language, sex, violence, drug use, etc.
  • Missi Tessier, the spokeswoman for the executive secretariat of the TV Parental Guidelines Monitoring Board, said "some 96% of parents polled said they were satisfied with the accuracy of the parental ratings for shows on television."

Movies and shows are rated unfairly

  • Having material go through the rating system is optional
  • A fee is required if the rating system is desired
  • Votes of the CARA must have a two-thirds majority vote
  • Of 17 shows, a total of 323 episodes, four rating catagories (TV-Y7, TV-PG, TV-14, TV-MA):
  1. 70% of the episodes had violence
  2. 53% of the episodes had sex
  3. 58% of the episodes had alcohol
  4. 31% of the episodes had smoking
  • Of the TV-7Y shows, violence was present in 73% of the TV-Y7 shows

My thoughts

I believe most ratings for both movies and shows are fair. Hands down, ratings need to exist in order for young kids to remain innocent and prevent them from having nightmares. To play it safe, I believe a show or movie having a lower rating is safer, as there is no risk of inappropriate visuals. I am a very skiddish person, and would prefer to avoid any possibilty of having to stay up all night for a bad dream. On the contrary, I do believe there is a flip side. In the reverse, some parents may believe ratings are limiting their children and sometimes that is the case. A scene with a gun or any type of violence or inappropriate language may appear and some parents may freak out while others will believe it was an okay choice to show the movie or show to their children. One very controversial movie is Jaws. Jaws originally had many gruesome scenes. When trimmed down, the movie was able to get a PG rating. However, it was still believed the film should be at least PG-13; as do I. The opening scene consists of teenagers drinking, smoking, and making out; yet the movie was given a PG rating. In this case, I believe movies are too lightly rated.

TV Shows
Rating Meaning
TV-Y This program is designed to be appropriate for all children
TV-Y7 This program is designed for children age 7 and above
TV-G Programs suitable for all ages
TV-PG This program contains material that parents may find unsuitable for younger children
TV-14 This program contains some material that many parents would find unsuitable for children under 14 years of age
TV-MA This program is specifically designed to be viewed by adults and therefore may be unsuitable for children under 17
